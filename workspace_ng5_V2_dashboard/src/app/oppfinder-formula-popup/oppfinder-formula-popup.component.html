Opportunity Finder Formula
For On-Time Payments: (Actual Invoice Clearing Date is ‘On’ or ‘Before’ the Invoice Due Date)
For Payment Terms without Discounts: (NET60, NET 90, etc.)
  • Potential Savings if the User pays on Invoice Due Date = Cost of Capital % * Invoice Amount * (Invoice Due Date - Actual Invoice Clearing Date) / 365
  • Best Payment Date = Invoice Due Date
For Payment Terms with Discounts: (2%10NET60, 3%NET30, etc.)
Scenario 1 - When Actual Invoice Clearing Date is ‘After’ the Last Discount Date, greater of the below two Potential Savings is shown on the grid with the respective Best Payment Date:
a) Potential Savings if the User pays on Last Discount Date = ((Discount % * Invoice Amount) + (Cost of Capital % * Invoice Amount * Discount Days / 365)) - (Cost of Capital % * Invoice Amount * (Actual Invoice Clearing Date - Last Discount Date) / 365)
Best Payment Date = Last Discount Date
OR
b) Potential Savings if the User pays on Invoice Due Date = (Cost of Capital % * Invoice Amount * (Invoice Due Date - Actual Invoice Clearing Date) / 365) - (Discount % * Invoice Amount)
Best Payment Date = Invoice Due Date
Scenario 2 - When Actual Invoice Clearing Date is ‘On’ or ‘Before’ the Last Discount Date, greater of the below two Potential Savings is shown on the grid with the respective Best Payment Date:
a) Potential Savings if the User pays on Last Discount Date = Cost of Capital % * Invoice Amount * (Last Discount Date - Actual Invoice Clearing Date) / 365
Best Payment Date = Last Discount Date
OR
b) Potential Savings if the User pays on Invoice Due Date = (Cost of Capital % * Invoice Amount * (Invoice Due Date - Actual Invoice Clearing Date) / 365) - (Discount % * Invoice Amount)
Best Payment Date = Invoice Due Date
  • if a) = b) in any of the above 2 scenarios, then the Potential Savings and Best Payment Date corresponding to a) is shown on the grid as early payments help in improving relationship with the Suppliers.
For Late Payments: (Actual Invoice Clearing Date is ‘After’ the Invoice Due Date)
For Payment Terms without Discounts: (NET60, NET 90, etc.)
  • Potential Savings if the User pays on Invoice Due Date = (Penalty % * Invoice Amount) - (Cost of Capital % * Invoice Amount * (Actual Invoice Clearing Date - Invoice Due Date) / 365)
  • Best Payment Date = Invoice Due Date
For Payment Terms with Discounts: (2%10NET60, 3%NET30, etc.)
Greater of the below two Potential Savings is shown on the grid with the respective Best Payment Date:
a) Potential Savings if the User pays on Last Discount Date = (Discount % * Invoice Amount) + (Penalty % * Invoice Amount) - (Cost of Capital % * Invoice Amount * (Actual Invoice Clearing Date - Last Discount Date) / 365)
Best Payment Date = Last Discount Date
OR
b) Potential Savings if the User pays on Invoice Due Date = (Penalty % * Invoice Amount) - (Cost of Capital % * Invoice Amount * (Actual Invoice Clearing Date - Invoice Due Date) / 365)
Best Payment Date = Invoice Due Date
  • if a) = b) in any of the above 2 scenarios, then the Potential Savings and Best Payment Date corresponding to a) is shown on the grid as early payments help in improving relationship with the Suppliers
